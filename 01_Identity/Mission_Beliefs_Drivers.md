# 4 All Snacks — Mission, Beliefs, Drivers

**Source:** Founder canon, captured 2026-05-12. Pre-workshop. Subject to pressure-testing in the brand session.

---

## Core Mission

> Everyday food, upgraded. Easier on parents. Better for kids. Same shelves, same prices, same routines — quietly made better.

We're not building a wellness brand or a premium brand, nor are we asking families to change anything. We're improving the food kids already reach for — the freeze pop, the fruit snack, the chocolate milk.

Same shelves. Same prices. Same routines.

The parent doesn't have to *decide* to be healthier. They buy what they were already going to buy, and it just happens to work.

---

## Three Brand Beliefs

1. **Living well shouldn't cost a premium.**
2. **The parent shouldn't have to be a hero.**
3. **The kid is the boss of the kid.**

---

## Brand Identity Drivers

- **Bold**
- **Heart on sleeve**
- **Approachable / plainspoken**
- **Fun**

---

## Workshop notes — what's still open

- The **"why"** — the line a doctor, nurse, teacher, parent says back unprompted: *"I buy this brand because ___."* Founder's working frame is *permission upgrade* / *permission to enjoy* but flagged it isn't landing yet.
- How **for all** as a platform name carries across SKUs without locking the brand into a single visual lane (so packaging can iterate SKU-to-SKU without breaking the system).
- Whether 4 All Snacks reads as a brand or a *holding* (the General Mills question — is the parent a brand, or are the SKUs the brands?).
- The donation pillar — what "for all = donate to hungry children" actually looks like in practice (not yet defined).
