## Reading the matrix — what jumps out

### The Walmart-only / Stockup / Solo / Heritage / In-store quadrant (top-left corner)
- **Maria, Donna, Pat** all cluster here.
- This is the **deepest-loyalty, hardest-to-reach-digitally** segment.
- They buy on shelf, they read paper circulars, they trust legacy brands.
- This is who the **reverse-mullet back-of-pack copy** has to win.
- Promotional levers: Sunday flyer, Rollback yellow, EBT/SNAP signaling, in-store sampling.

### The Multi-retailer / Fill-in / Solo / Trend / App-first quadrant (bottom-right corner)
- **Jess and Megan** anchor here.
- Highest-income, highest-digital-fluency, most-likely-to-discover-through-content.
- App-first promotional mechanics (Walmart+ exclusive pricing, product-detail-page ingredient transparency, creator-led discovery) win this segment.
- They might never see the shelf. Front-of-pack design matters less; the app product-detail-page matters more.
- **They are NOT the primary launch buyer at Tier 1 stockup volume.** They are critical for *brand discovery, social signal, and content amplification* — but the basket math runs through Maria/Donna/Pat.

### The Family-group / Between / Between quadrant (middle)
- **Garcias, Ron, Kim+Cal** sit here.
- They shop *with people* — kids, partners, grandkids. The purchase moment is a *social event,* not a private optimization.
- Decision-making is distributed: kid picks, parent approves; grandmother trusts, grandkid points.
- The **reverse-mullet packaging principle is built for this middle quadrant.** Kid-front, parent-back, satisfaction at both ends of the buying motion.

## Strategic implications

### Tier 1 launch buyer concentration
The four personas with the **highest Tier-1 (Value Stock-Up, Walmart, multi-pack)** purchase probability:

1. **Maria** — buys what's affordable and trustworthy
2. **Donna** — buys what's legacy and value-stacked
3. **Garcias** — buys what's family-event-appropriate and bilingual-family-trustworthy
4. **Kim + Cal** — buys what's friction-free and family-pack-priced

The other four (Jess, Pat, Megan, Ron) are **secondary or episodic** for Tier-1 launch volume.

### Voice has to land for Maria + Donna + Garcias + Kim/Cal simultaneously
- **Plain-language** (Maria, Donna, Pat, Kim/Cal)
- **Bilingual / multi-generational respectful** (Garcias)
- **Bipartisan** (Maria + Donna live in different political tribes; Garcias in another; Kim/Cal in yet another — the voice can't tribal-code)
- **Treat-language friendly** (Kim/Cal, Maria)
- **Multi-pack value math forward** (all four)

### Front-of-pack design has to land for the **kid in the cart** (all family-group personas) AND **the parent doing the shelf scan in 2 seconds** (Maria, Donna, Pat)

The kid is the picker for Garcias, Kim/Cal, sometimes Ron. The parent is the picker for Maria, Donna, Pat (when kid not present), Jess (digital), Megan (digital + Aldi). The reverse-mullet is designed for the moments when **both are present** — kid points, parent reads back, deal closed.

### Promotional channel split
- **Sunday circular + in-store endcap:** Maria, Donna, Pat. (Highest-LTV, hardest-to-reach digitally.)
- **Walmart+ app + product-detail-page copy:** Jess, Megan.
- **YouTube / TikTok / WhatsApp:** Garcias (kid + cultural channels), Megan (creator-led), Kim/Cal (background scroll), Ron (sports-creator-adjacent).
- **Sports-channel sponsorship:** Ron primarily, Garcias secondarily, Kim/Cal tertiarily.
- **In-store sampling:** All eight, but disproportionately effective for Maria (immediate feed-the-kid moment).

## How this feeds the workshop

**Block 03 (Insights):**
The matrix shows the **structural tension** the brand has to resolve — it has to play in three quadrants (top-left for stockup volume, middle for family-group reverse-mullet, bottom-right for content amplification) without picking one and alienating the others.

**Block 04 (Territories):**
A territory that lands top-left + middle but alienates bottom-right is a *Liquid Death analogue* — bipartisan-mass-loud-confident. A territory that lands middle + bottom-right but loses top-left is *Bobbie / Cerebelly* — wellness-coded premium. A territory that lands all three is the workshop's actual job.

**Block 12 (Pre-Launch Strategy):**
- Pre-launch audience-build is **mostly bottom-right** (Jess + Megan + the content-amplifier corner of Garcias and Ron). They're the ones reachable before product hits shelf.
- Launch-moment activation is **top-left + middle** (the Walmart shelf, the family-group purchase moments).
- Chase Dimond + Depesh + Indigo design the bridge from one to the other.
