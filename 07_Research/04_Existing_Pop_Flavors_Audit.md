## Strategic read

### Why this matters
- **The Big Six** is a 1969 white-American suburban-mom flavor lexicon. Mango chamoy, agua fresca, dragonfruit, chamoy, Tajín — those flavors are **already in the kid's mouth** at Hispanic-household scale (Multi-Gen Garcias persona), at TikTok-trend scale (Korean-Mexican-fusion content, Asian-fruit content), and at convenience-store scale (De La Calle, Jarritos, Topo Chico).
- The category incumbents are **selling a flavor lexicon that the modern kid has already outgrown.**
- This is **flavor innovation as competitive moat.** Calcium and Vitamin D are the "good in" substantiation; mango chamoy is the *kid will actually ask Mom for this* substantiation.

### Hispanic crossover as a strategic vector
- Davis raised this on the 5/12 call: *"What if you did like a mango chamoy flavor or like a tamarind lime? Gringos AND Hispanic families would like it."*
- The deck's flavor list confirms it — three of five proposed flavors (watermelon agua fresca, mango chamoy, spicy peach with chamoy/Tajín DNA) are explicitly Hispanic-crossover.
- This is **not a Hispanic-targeted SKU.** This is a *flavor system that lands in 100% of households* and over-indexes in Hispanic households without alienating any other persona. Bipartisan-by-design.

### Flavor architecture for the SKU lineup
The workshop should resolve:

- **Launch flavor count.** 4 (matches De La Calle box), 5 (matches the deck list), 6 (matches Otter Pop pack-of-12 with 6 distinct), 8 (matches Kirkland box)?
- **Flavor naming.** "Mango Chamoy" vs. "Spicy Mango" vs. "Mango Picante." Naming has to read for the kid AND the parent AND be merchandiseable on shelf in 4-color print.
- **Pack-format flavor distribution.** 100-count Costco box vs. 12-count Walmart box vs. 6-count grab pack — does flavor count contract with pack size, or stay constant?

### The flavors that should probably *survive* from the legacy six
- **Strawberry** — universal kid favorite, can be reimagined as "Strawberry Passionfruit" (deck) without losing the legacy red-pop visual cue.
- **Grape** — strong nostalgia for the parent who's buying; consider keeping a "Concord Grape" SKU as a bridge.
- The others (Lime / Fruit Punch / Berry / Orange) — drop or radically reframe. They're flavor-coded as "the cheap pop in the white-plastic tube."
