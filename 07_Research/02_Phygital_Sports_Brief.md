# 4 All Snacks — Phygital Sports Brief

**Source:** Ishan + Elliot strategy-session input deck, slides 30-31. Captured 2026-05-18.

This is the **support-pack research** the deck includes on how kids consume sports today — physical, digital, hybrid ("phygital"). Critical input to **Block 03 (Insights)** and **Block 12 (Pre-Launch Strategy)** — sports partnerships and the school-bus-to-sideline route are surfacing as the most promising distribution mechanic.

---

## "Phygital" sports — U.S. trending

Three lanes kids are crossing freely between:

### 1. Traditional sports
- **Basketball** — content-driven (highlight clips, NBA TikTok, AAU influencer culture)
- **Soccer** — first sport for most U.S. kids
- **Flag football** — fastest growing youth sport

### 2. Esports
- **Roblox & Minecraft** — digital playgrounds (the "third place")
- **Fortnite & Valorant** — cultural crossovers (skins, dances, sponsorship deals)

### 3. Extreme sports
- **Skateboarding** — Olympic debut has led to skyrocketing popularity among suburban kids; content-ready
- **Climbing** — lifestyle and social alternative; gym membership growth

---

## "Phygital" sports — U.S. by the numbers

1. **Over 20% of kids choose their favorite sports team before turning seven.** (Brand-affinity lock-in happens earlier than category-affinity.)
2. **71% of U.S. kids aged 5-15 log onto YouTube daily,** with nearly half **actively seeking content about their favorite sports teams.**
3. **53% of kids discover new brands in online spaces.** **65% report learning about new things from influencers.**

---

## Strategic implications for 4 All Snacks

### Distribution mechanic
- **Sideline / post-game snack distribution** is the highest-leverage category entry point Davis flagged on the 5/12 call. The phygital data validates it: sports is where the kid spends mindshare, where the parent buys for the kid's social context, and where the snack format (single-serve, shareable, low-mess) is structurally favored over chips/cereal.
- The trial mechanic: **first snack you give to someone in a bad economy.** Free or sample-able at the field. Doesn't feel like begging, feels like generosity. The mom doesn't feel bad giving away an extra one because "it's just a freeze pop." Compounds into a household stock-up purchase the next Walmart trip.

### Format implication
- **Skateboarding, climbing, esports** = aesthetic permission for **loud color, bold graphics, irreverent humor.** These are not Whole Foods aesthetics. They're the Death / De La Calle / Liquid Death visual language Davis already flagged as the right register.
- **Soccer & flag football** = the parent-supervised, snack-bag, every-Saturday-morning routine. The product needs to survive a cooler, a soccer mom's car, a sideline distribution moment.

### Sponsorship / partnership lane
- **AAU basketball, Little League, youth flag football, local skate parks, climbing gyms** — hyperlocal, low-cost, high-trust sampling routes that bypass the mass-media gatekeeping that the brand can't afford pre-launch.
- **YouTube + creator content** is where the kid's brand discovery happens. **Influencer learning at 65%** means a single resonant kid-creator partnership can move more category awareness than a TV spot for the same money.

## How this feeds the workshop

**Block 03 (Insights):**
Sport-as-CEP (Category Entry Point) is one of the strongest behavioral insights the deck surfaces. Shotton + Ferrier work this thread — what's the cue (sideline whistle, post-game), what's the routine (parent reaches for snack bag), what's the reward (kid is hot, sweaty, needs cold/sweet/quick) — and reverse-engineers the position into the routine.

**Block 12 (Pre-Launch Strategy):**
Pre-launch audience build (Ishan's afternoon ask) almost certainly runs **through sports + creator + sampling routes,** not paid social. The phygital data argues against a Meta-first pre-launch and toward a *seeded-at-the-field* + *YouTube-creator-amplified* play. Julian + Chase Dimond + Depesh stress-test this in the room.
