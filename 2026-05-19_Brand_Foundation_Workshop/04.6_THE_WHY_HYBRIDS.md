## ROUNDTABLE — each voice picks their lead from V1-V4

**JULIAN COLE** *(strategic synthesis):*

> "**V1 + V2 paired.** V1 is the cleanest narrative arc for a Joe + Walmart-buyer pitch — it positions the company as a generational succession, which is *investable.* V2 supplies the freshest strategic IP — *'we think about the whole childhood'* is genuinely new territory no incumbent or BFY upstart owns. V3's intellectually beautiful but might read too abstract upstream. V4's consumer-warm but won't move Joe alone. V1 + V2 is the strongest core pair."

**JOHN HEGARTY** *(creative gate, Big Idea instincts):*

> "**V2 — specifically the phrase *'we think about the whole childhood.'*** That's the closest sentence to a Big Idea seed in the entire WHY set. It does what a Big Idea has to do: state a posture *(we think differently)* + name the dimension we own *(the whole childhood)* + imply a structural advantage *(everyone else thinks per-snack)*. If the founders lock this phrase as the WHY's anchor, every downstream brand move gets easier."

**JON STEEL** *(narrative / consumer truth):*

> "Emotionally — **V1 + V3.** V1 carries the most genuine *emotional* truth — *'childhood we still get to shape'* lands because it names a thing parents are quietly aware of but never hear back. V3 is the parent's real psychological reality — every parent makes hundreds of small choices and is anxious that none of them matter. The Nike-shoes archetype tells them they do. *V1 + V3 is the most emotionally resonant pair for parent-direct copy.*"

**ED MCCABE** *(language / writability):*

> "Pure writability — **V1.** The phrase *'the kids whose entire childhood we still get to shape'* is a sentence I could write to forever — every comm can start with *'for the children whose...'* and finish with whatever the truth of the moment is. V2's *'we think about the whole childhood'* is the second-strongest single phrase. V3 has the best *close* (*'smallest decision, biggest payoff'*). V4 has the best *tagline raw material* (*'a win for the kid, the parent, the family'*). **For a hybrid, weave V1 + V2 + V3 + V4 phrases together — they're already complementary, the room just needs the right grammar.**"

**RORY SUTHERLAND** *(counterintuitive):*

> "**V2 and V3 are the distinctive ones.** V2's counterintuitive at the structural level *(think childhood, not snack)*. V3's counterintuitive at the meta-level *(small acts compound into life-change — most parents under-use this frame because it feels too gentle to matter)*. V1's beautiful but expected — *every brand says 'we shape your kid.'* V4's even more expected — *'win for everyone'* is a brand-strategy cliché unless executed sharply. **The zag is V2 + V3.** If the agency can build a hybrid that leads with the structural-reframe (V2) and lands with the compounding-payoff (V3), that's the most distinctive WHY."

## 5 HYBRID COMBINATIONS

Each hybrid is a complete self-contained WHY statement built from the best phrases across V1-V4. Variety on length, tone, and lead emphasis so the founders can feel which mix lands.

---

### **HYBRID 1 — THE BALANCED CORE (Agency #1 recommendation)**

> **The kids' snack aisle was built for 1990 kids. We're rebuilding it for the kids whose entire childhood we still get to shape — because we think about the whole childhood, not just one snack at a time. Better snacks, compounded across every day of their growing up. Same shelf. Same price. A win for the kid. A win for the parent. A win for the family.**

*Combines:* V1 generational opener · V1 emotional core *(childhood we still get to shape)* · V2 freshest phrase *(we think about the whole childhood)* · V1 middle *(compounded across every day)* · V1 parity beat *(same shelf, same price)* · V1+V4 win triplet close.

*Strongest single hybrid for the Joe + Walmart-buyer pitch deck.* Cleanest narrative, all the favorite phrases preserved, length feels deck-readable.

---

### **HYBRID 2 — THE INTELLECTUAL ZAG** *(Sutherland-recommended)*

> **Most kid-snack brands think about one snack at a time. We think about the whole childhood. Five-plus snacks a day across twelve years compounds — and every small choice a parent makes adds up to a meaningfully different kid. Same shelf. Same price. The smallest decision. The biggest payoff.**

*Combines:* V2 opener *(think about the whole childhood)* · V2 math · V3 emotional-unlock *(small choice adds up)* · V1 parity beat · V3 closer *(smallest decision, biggest payoff)*.

*Most distinctive zag.* If the founders want the WHY to live in *fresh strategic territory* rather than *cleanest narrative,* this is the choice. Less consumer-warm than H1; more business-thesis defensible.

---

### **HYBRID 3 — THE FAMILY-WARM** *(Steel-recommended)*

> **Raising a kid is hard enough without negotiating snacks. We're rebuilding the snacks you used to grab — for the kids whose entire childhood you still get to shape. Same shelf. Same price. A win for the kid. A win for the parent. A win for the family.**

*Combines:* V4 opener *(raising a kid is hard enough)* · V1 product-truth *(rebuilding the snacks)* · V1 emotional core *(childhood we still get to shape)* · V1 parity · V4 win triplet.

*Warmest, most parent-direct.* Best at the Facebook-mom-group / parent-recommendation register. **Most likely to be quoted back by a mom in the wild.** Less business-strategic than H1 or H2.

---

### **HYBRID 4 — THE PARENT-EMPOWERMENT** *(Nike-Chipotle archetype, Elliot's unlock)*

> **Every parent makes hundreds of small choices for their kid every week. We exist to turn one of them — the snack — into a choice that compounds. Better snacks at every part of the day. Same shelf. Same price. The smallest decision. The biggest payoff — across a childhood we still get to shape, for a family that wins every time.**

*Combines:* V3 archetype opener *(small choices)* · V3 mechanism *(choice that compounds)* · V2 platform spine *(every part of the day)* · V1 parity · V3 closer · V1 emotional core *(childhood we still get to shape)* · V4 win register *(family that wins)*.

*Most parent-empowering frame.* Treats the parent as the protagonist (their choice matters; their daily choice compounds). **Best for the Nike-shoes / Chipotle-burrito archetype Elliot named.** Slightly longer.

---

### **HYBRID 5 — THE COMPRESSED PUNCH** *(brand-name-anchored)*

> **The kids' snack aisle hasn't changed in 35 years. We changed it — for the kids whose entire childhood we still get to shape. Small snacks. Big days. Same shelf. Same price. A win every family agrees on.**

*Combines:* V1 historical opener (35 years) · V1 emotional core · "Small snacks. Big days." (the brand-name resonance from Block 06) · V1 parity · V4 win-language compressed.

*Tightest version.* Most marketing-coded — almost reads as a brand manifesto opener. **Lands the Big Days brand-name candidate directly** — if Big Days wins as parent-brand, this hybrid becomes the natural launch-manifesto opener.
