## THE LOCKED PLATFORM (compressed)

**The brand is a *Crew of characters* who live in a *Headquarters / Snack-Clubhouse / Lab.*** The Crew shows up across every part of the kid's day — especially in the *in-between moments* (between meals, between school and home, between kid-energy and parent-bandwidth). HQ produces *Drops* (numbered, hyped, collectible limited-editions) that kids anticipate.

**Davis's emotional anchor for the world:** *"Characters that live in between moments — between lunch and dinner, the different parts of the day. One of the most fun times for kids. One of the hardest times for parents."* — That's the in-between moment the brand owns.

**Content/social layer:** Make-a-Face. Every Crew member, every Drop, every flavor generates face-content (brain-freeze, tongue-color, surprise) for TikTok/YouTube-shorts native engagement.

## WORLD MECHANICS (what the platform generates regardless of name)

These are the *operating mechanics* the merged Crew + HQ platform produces. They land regardless of which name wins:

### 1. **The Drops** (limited-edition / hype-cycle)

HQ produces *Drops* — numbered, dated, hype-coded limited editions. Each Drop introduces:
- A new flavor (sometimes mainstream, sometimes seasonal-cultural — Mango Chamoy at Hispanic Heritage Month, Tropical Lemonade at Memorial Day, etc.)
- A new Crew member (or guest Crew)
- A pack-design variant (collectible)
- A content cycle (kid-anticipation on social → in-store → playground)

Kids resonate with *drops* (sneaker culture, gaming-skin drops, Roblox-drops). The brand inherits the energy without copying the format.

### 2. **The Crew** (recurring characters across SKUs)

Each launch SKU/flavor is tied to a Crew character:
- Strawberry = [Crew member name with strawberry-coded personality]
- Grape = [Crew member, grape-coded]
- Cherry = [Crew member, cherry-coded]
- Tropical = [Crew member, tropical-coded]
- (Etc. for Wave 2 fruit snack, Wave 3 chip, Wave 4 cereal — each gets its Crew)

The Crew has personalities the kids learn. They have catchphrases. They have *in-between-moment* habits (where they appear, what they say). They are speakable — kid quotes them on the playground.

### 3. **HQ Inhabitants** (the non-Crew supporting cast)

Davis liked *snack engineer* and *snack detective.* Possible inhabitants:
- **The Snack Engineer** *(invents the flavors)*
- **The Snack Detective** *(uncovers the next flavor — sets up Drops)*
- **The Cold-Storage Keeper** *(runs the freezer wing)*
- **The Pack-Designer** *(makes the SKUs look the way they look)*
- **The Test Kitchen Crew** *(rotating cameos)*

These appear in ad-world, social content, and Wave-2+ pack stories — they don't go on the launch packs but populate the brand world.

### 4. **The In-Between Moments** (where the world lives)

The Crew/HQ shows up where the *in-between moments* live:
- After school, before dinner
- Between lunch and the after-school chase
- Between the parent's "5 more minutes" and the actual end of those 5 minutes
- Between leaving home and arriving at the soccer field
- Between Roblox and homework

Brand world = the secret club of *these moments.* The kid feels claimed by it. The parent feels their hardest hour of the day gets a partner.

### 5. **Flavor Naming** (Olly-translation logic applied to Crew/HQ)

Two options:
- **Option A (Crew-led):** flavors named for Crew members — *Grape Knuckles, Strawberry Slim, Cherry Joe.*
- **Option B (HQ-led):** flavors named for *what they do* — *Brain-Freeze Berry, Tongue-Stain Grape, Wide-Awake Tropical, Lights-Out Cherry.*
- **Option C (Hybrid):** Crew name + outcome — *Knuckles' Brain-Freeze Grape* / *Slim's Tongue-Stain Strawberry.*

### 6. **Surface lines** (tagline-tier — emerge from the platform, not from the 30 archived)

These are *surfaces* of the Crew+HQ platform, not the Big Idea itself. The Big Idea is *the world / the Crew / the in-between moments.* Possible surfaces:

## NAMING THE BRAND — the big decision

The merged Crew+HQ direction unlocks the question: ***what's the parent-brand name?*** Block 06 was scheduled to run this debate; the platform makes it more urgent.

The candidates that emerge from this brainstorm + Davis's seeds + the constraints (no Honest Foods proximity, no "kids" in name, kid-quotable, Kinder-shape scalability):

### Strongest candidates (room's first read):

1. **CABOODLE** — Davis already likes. Singular, kid-fun, made-up-feeling, doesn't anchor to category. Easy to defend ("the whole caboodle = everything kids want"). Generative across SKUs.

2. **RASCALS** — connects to Little Rascals nostalgia (parent-warm) + mischief energy (kid-fun) + Davis already gestured at it. Could risk a Trouble-flavored read.

3. **SNACKQUARTERS** — fun mashup, owns the HQ frame literally. Slightly punny but kid-friendly. Generative (HQ structure built-in).

4. **THE STASH** — confident, kid-coded, doesn't say "snack" or "kid" but reads as both. Generative (every SKU is "in the stash").

5. **DOOZY** — kid-vocab, single-word, fun-sounding, distinctive. "Pop a doozy" / "have a doozy" / "got a doozy?" 

### Weaker but worth flagging:

- *Rasp Pack* (Davis's seed) — too narrow for a brand name; better as a *Crew* name inside a different parent brand
- *Whatnots / Doohickey* — too cute, might not scale
- *Spree / Caper / Junket* — old-words, less kid-natural

### Killed:

- Anything with "Snack" *as the brand name* (Snackquarters allowed because it's mashup; "Snack Co." / "Snack Pack" / "SnackHQ" too generic)
- Anything close to *"Honest"* / *"Real"* (per Davis's earlier 4-All-Snacks-too-close-to-Honest-Foods directive)
- Any cute-coastal-coded word (Wonder, Marvel, Joy, Bliss, Glow, etc.)
