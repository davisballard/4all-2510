## PART A — VOICE RULES (DRAFT, FOR LIVE EDIT)

### Voice in one line

**The brand speaks like a parent who noticed talking to another parent who noticed.**

Plain. Confident. Not preaching. Not flattering. Not lecturing. Not winking. Acknowledging. The brand assumes the parent already knows what the brand knows; we just say it out loud and remove the catch.

### Five voice rules

#### Rule 1 — Plain English. Always.

- No category jargon. No *clean-label, micronutrient-density, anti-inflammatory, optimal nutrition, full-spectrum.*
- 5th-grade reading level for everything customer-facing. (Back-of-pack supplement facts excepted — those are regulatory.)
- One-syllable words where possible. *Same. Real. Yes. Better.*
- ***Vocab pool (research) — write toward these:*** *"bouncing off the walls" (the universal Red 40 phrase) · "ingredients we can't pronounce" (parent-active vocab, NOT "synthetic dyes") · "peacekeeping mission" / "lifesaver" (parental-labor framing, not indulgence). See `07_Research/10_Parent_Language_In_The_Wild.md`.*
- McCabe sentence test: read each line aloud. If it sounds like a brand brief, kill it.

#### Rule 2 — Acknowledge, don't preach.

- The parent already knows the system is broken. We don't explain it. We just *show that we know they know.*
- DO: *"You noticed. So did we."*
- DON'T: *"Did you know that snack foods marketed to kids are full of artificial colors and excessive sugar?"*
- The brand is the consumer's *coworker,* not their teacher.

#### Rule 3 — No charity coding.

- Maria's hard line. *We're proud of being resourceful — don't pity us.*
- DO: *"For the parents doing the work without the extra credit."*
- DON'T: *"We believe every family deserves access to healthy food."*
- The brand never positions itself as *helping* the consumer. The brand *removes the catch* the consumer was navigating.

#### Rule 4 — Bipartisan-by-design. No tribal coding.

- Donna's hard line meets Megan's hard line. The brand cannot pattern-match to either tribe's visual or verbal codes.
- DO: *"Same shelf you've been shopping. Quietly better."*
- DON'T: *"Real food for real families."* (MAHA-coded)
- DON'T: *"Conscious snacking for the next generation."* (coastal-wellness-coded)
- Test every line: would Donna read this without flinching? Would Megan? If either flinches, rewrite.

#### Rule 5 — Quietly. Always quietly.

- The Hegarty territory that became the brand's signature register. The brand never *announces* its goodness. It just *is* better.
- DO: *"With calcium and vitamin D."*
- DON'T: *"With essential calcium AND clinically-supported vitamin D!"*
- DO: *"Tastes like an Otter Pop. With more in it."*
- DON'T: *"BETTER FOR YOU than the leading freeze pop!!!"*
- The brand never shouts at the consumer about its goodness. The consumer hears about it from the shelf, the price tag, the kid's mouth, the back-of-pack shield. The brand stays quiet on the *quality* and louder on the *flavor.*

### The brand's tonal range

The brand can move across this range (situation-dependent):

| Mode | When | Example line |
|---|---|---|
| **Plainspoken-statement** | Default — back-of-pack, mission, About page | *Same shelf. Same price. Quietly better.* |
| **Knowing-conspiratorial** | Social / brand-voice content | *You noticed. So did we.* |
| **Kid-confident** | Front-of-pack flavor naming, kid-facing | *Mango Chamoy.* (the flavor IS the line) |
| **Receipt-language** | Substantiation, USDA citations, ingredient transparency | *With calcium, vitamin D, and vitamin E. Same calories. Same price.* |
| **Restraint-humor** | Occasional brand-voice flourishes | *We didn't reinvent the freeze pop. We just updated what's in it.* |

### The brand's tonal *no-go* range

| Mode | Why we don't go there |
|---|---|
| **Wellness performance** | Violates We-Are-NOT guardrail |
| **Charity-coded** | Maria flinches |
| **Coastal-elite-knowing** | Donna flinches |
| **Tokenized cultural** | Garcias grandmother withdraws veto |
| **Excessive humor** | Liquid Death lane; not our register; Maria + Pat don't respond |
| **Long-form storytelling** | Kim + Cal don't have bandwidth |
| **Authoritative-medical** | Saul gates anything that lapses into FDA-disclaimer territory |
| **Profanity** | Walmart kid-snack tier — out |
| **Adult-coded irony** | Confuses kid-buyer test |
| **MAHA-coded** | Tribal coding; alienates Megan + portions of Garcias |
| **Anti-Big-Food explicit** | Tribal coding the other direction; alienates Donna's brand loyalties |

**MCCABE:**
> "First draft. Let me walk through what each section is doing.
>
> Opening paragraph: *For the parent who noticed* + four *you noticed* lines + *So did we.* — establishes the POV, the addressee, and the empathy. Builds toward a beat.
>
> Middle paragraph: *We didn't reinvent the freeze pop* + the *same shelf, same price, same kid* triplet + the specific upgrades (calcium, vitamin D, flavor lexicon, packaging) + *no premium, no permission slip, no catch.* — articulates the promise.
>
> Identity paragraph: *We are not a wellness brand. We are not a premium brand. We are the parent who noticed, talking to another parent who noticed.* — locks the brand's self-definition.
>
> Sign-off: *Same shelf. Same price. Quietly better. Finally.* — three locked elements (positioning + emotion) as the manifesto's final line. This is the line Ishan reads aloud and the line that lives on the About page."
