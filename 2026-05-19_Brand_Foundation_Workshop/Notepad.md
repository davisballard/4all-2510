INSIGHT C2-1 — WELLNESS DIDN'T FAIL THESE PARENTS. IT REFUSED TO SERVE THEM.

  The mass-retail parent didn't fail to choose better. The wellness category effectively
  opted itself out of mass households over 15 years. Priced Maria + Costco-migrant out.
  Tokenized Garcias. Patronized Donna. Demanded cognitive work Kim+Cal don't have at 8pm.
  Parents are smart, paying attention, and have already made the rational decision that
  wellness isn't for them.

  Why it's deep: inverts the brand's job. Most BFY brands position as educators. This
  says: the parents don't need education — they need acknowledgment.

  Big-Idea implication: confident-and-warm tone, never educational. "We're here" not "let
  us teach you."

  INSIGHT C2-2 — SMART IS A MATH PROBLEM, NOT A FEELING.

  Elliot named "feel smart." Davis quantified what smart MEANS — Maria: "one purchase
  replaces two." Costco-migrant: "macros at parity price." Same math, two read-modes.
  Brand's job: make the math LEGIBLE so the parent does it themselves.

  Four-touchpoint validation (shelf → price → taste → label) = four wins the parent
  calculates on their own. The brand never CLAIMS smart. It gives the parent the
  components to BE smart.

  Why it's deep: shifts smart from emotion to agency. Transparency-as-mechanism made
  concrete.

  Big-Idea implication: the pack and voice show the work, don't perform the conclusion.

  ---
  INSIGHT C2-3 — THE KID-CART INFLUENCE DIDN'T DIE. THE CHANNELS MIGRATED.

  The room worried digital shopping killed kid-influence. Wrong. 60%+ of demo still has
  kid-driven purchase influence. The mechanism didn't weaken — it relocated to: hanging on
   mom's elbow during Instacart · asking by name after YouTube/school/TikTok · eating it
  at a friend's house then demanding it at home. Kid-channel courtship is co-equal with
  parent-channel — and often the FIRST encounter.

  Why it's deep: reframes a defeated assumption as a fully-alive-and-just-moved mechanism.
   Most BFY brands under-invested in kid-channel because they assumed Instacart killed it.
   They were wrong.

  Big-Idea implication: the brand's voice lands equally with kids and parents. Block 12
  designs both tracks at equal weight.

  ---
  INSIGHT C2-4 — THE BINARY ISN'T BETTER-VS-FUN. IT'S BOTH-OR-NEITHER.

  The category has been forcing a false binary: organic-no-fun-no-flavor (Annie's, Honest,
   Pedialyte) OR fun-no-better-for-you (Otter Pops, Capri-Sun, Gushers). The parent isn't
  failing to choose better. The parent is refusing to give up fun. Goldfish wins because
  Annie's lost the fun. Otter Pops wins because parents won't sacrifice kid-joy for
  nutrition.

  Kid-joy and parent-permission BOTH have to be intact. The category has been forcing a
  trade-off the parents have correctly refused.

  Why it's deep: names the parent's BFY-resistance as rational and protective, not
  failure-to-care. The category is the problem, not the parent.

  Big-Idea implication: the brand never gives up the fun. Never. Anti-pattern gate across
  Block 07/08/11.
