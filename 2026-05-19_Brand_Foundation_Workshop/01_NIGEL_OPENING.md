## BEAT 4 — NIGEL PROPOSES THE SESSION STRUCTURE

**Four variety levers — Nigel picks case by case. Then he announces the steps explicitly.**

---

**NIGEL:**
> "Format: **Round table per block.** Experts debate, 2-3 directions surface per generative block, the founders pick one. Discipline: no block ends without a *decision* — *parked* counts, *killed* counts, *locked* counts. *Maybe* doesn't.
>
> Opener: I'm opening, then handing the chair to whoever leads that block. After Beat 4 I step out of the lead seat unless commercial-viability comes up — in which case I'll interject with a MODE-A verdict (GREEN / AMBER / RED).
>
> Discovery timing: low ambiguity. The deck did most of the discovery. The two open items I flagged in Beat 2 — Natural Rascal lock status, donation pillar — we surface in Block 02. We don't pre-game them now.
>
> Echo style: paraphrase + the verbatim sentence I just quoted. Long brief, full deck. Pull quotes preserved through Block 02.
>
> **Here's how we'll run the workshop:**
>
> **Day 1:**
>
> 1. *(this block)* — I assemble the room, propose the structure, hand the chair.
> 2. **Block 02 Audit (45 min)** — Julian + the room read the deck back. What's locked, what's open, where's the brand sitting. Surface the two open items (Natural Rascal, donation pillar). Founders react.
> 3. **Block 03 Insights (90 min)** — Julian + Steel pull the foundational insight out of the audit. 4Cs + Steel deprivation + Shotton behavioral lenses + a guest Hispanic-American voice for the Garcias persona work. Lock a primary tension frame.
> 4. **Block 04 Territories (75 min)** — Hegarty proposes 3 territories from the insight. Room debates. Converge to 2 for Big Idea consideration.
> 5. *Lunch.*
> 6. **Block 05 Big Idea (120 min)** — Julian + Hegarty co-chair. Each strategist + creative voice proposes a Big Idea candidate. Stress-test against the territories, against the four-parity model, against the personas. Lock one.
> 7. **Block 06 Brand Name (60 min)** — McCabe runs 4All vs Natural Rascal vs new. Stress-test each name against Maria, Garcias, Megan, Ron at minimum. Decision lock.
> 8. **Block 07 Aesthetic World (60 min)** — Fink + Massimo lock the visual register. Loud-wall, reverse-mullet system, typographic lane. Sketch where the brand sits between De La Calle / Liquid Death / Vacation / Mighty Fundippa.
> 9. **Block 08 Voice + Manifesto (60 min)** — McCabe writes the voice rules, drafts the manifesto live with the room.
> 10. **Block 09 Day 1 Wrap (30 min)** — me + Julian. Lock check. What's open for Day 2. Overnight assignments — Ishan and Elliot read what we locked, sit with it, come back fresh.
>
> **Day 2:**
>
> 11. **Block 10 Day 2 Open (20 min)** — me digesting overnight reactions, setting Day 2 priorities.
> 12. **Block 11 Identity + Packaging (60 min)** — Massimo + Fink sketch the SKU-1 Ice Pop packaging direction. Front / back layout under the reverse-mullet rule. Color block, typography call, character/no-character call.
> 13. **Block 12 Pre-Launch Strategy (90 min)** — Julian + Chase + Depesh + Indigo design the pre-launch audience build. The bottom-right persona quadrant (Jess + Megan + content-amplifier corners of Garcias/Ron) becomes the pre-launch audience. The seeded-at-the-field + YouTube-creator-amplified play vs. paid-social-first. Sports CEPs from the phygital brief.
> 14. **Block 13 Wrap + 8-Doc Preview (40 min)** — me + Julian. Decisions log. Preview the Campaign Book — Strategy Brief, Audience Brief, Creative Brief, Comms Brief, Channel Plan, Hook Compliance Grid, Production Brief, Measurement. That's the next-phase deliverable, not in scope this week, but Ishan walks into Kinder's knowing it exists.
>
> Two days. Thirteen blocks. One Big Idea. One name. One aesthetic. One voice. One pre-launch sketch. One foundation Ishan can defend in front of Kinder's principal."
