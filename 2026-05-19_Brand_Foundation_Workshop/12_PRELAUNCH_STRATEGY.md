### 1. Persona-tightening as a deliverable (Davis, verbatim)

→ **Post-workshop deliverable** in the Campaign Book stack: refined persona profiles with drivers/barriers per persona, plus candidate additions (e.g., the **$100k+ Walmart-shopper-migrant** Davis flagged). Block 12 references the existing eight as the *starting* set; the tightened set ships with the post-Kinder's Campaign Book.

### 2. Trial-risk-for-Maria → sampling architecture is load-bearing (Davis + Elliot, verbatim)

> *"For Maria — what's the cost if I get this wrong? $20 wasted = vitamins, healthcare, another snack, anything else. Part of the gain is a potential loss. The mass-retail value shopper looks for **consistency from what they know** — they'll put it back down if they think the kids won't eat it."*

→ **In-store sampling + sideline sampling + school-event sampling moves from *nice-to-have* to *load-bearing.*** Free trial = zero loss = breaks the trial-risk equation. Ferrier locked: this is the single largest lever for getting Maria + Donna + Garcias + Kim+Cal across the trial threshold. Block 12 designs the sampling architecture as Tier-1 priority, not Tier-3.

### 3. Functional-benefit visibility = content-strategy spine (Ishan, verbatim)

> *"For content strategy / marketing strategy — making sure the **awareness and profile of what we offer functionally is visible** matters a lot. To Davis's earlier point about transparency-of-functional-benefit."*

→ Pre-launch content has to **make the functional benefit visible BEFORE the parent picks the pack off the shelf.** Two channels carry this:

- **Kid channels** (YouTube creator kids, school events, sideline sampling, TikTok kid-fun creators) → the kid sees the pack at school/online, asks for it at home, *says the brand name* during Instacart-elbow-grab (per the 60%+ kid-influence stat Ishan pulled).
- **Parent channels** (parent-creator UGC, school-newsletter ads, TikTok-mom content, the *"holistic nutritional value"* comparison content) → parent encounters the brand via 1-3 trusted parent-voice impressions BEFORE first Walmart shelf encounter, lowering trial risk.

The four-touchpoint validation framework (Block 11) **starts before shelf** in pre-launch — *visual recognition + parent-creator trust signal* = the *fifth* and *sixth* touchpoints stacked in front of the shelf-pickup moment.

### 4. The two-value-narratives split (Davis, verbatim)

> *"$100k+ shopper looking at macros = benefit. Maria = necessity. Same nutrition, two reads."*

→ Pre-launch creative tests **both messages with both audiences** through paid-social variant testing — does the "replaces 2 purchases" narrative resonate with Maria? Does the "macros + clean nutrition at parity price" narrative resonate with the $100k+ migrant? Indigo + Depesh design the matrix.

### 5. The multi-channel platform mandate (Davis, verbatim)

> *"This has to go Walmart, Costco, Sam's Club, then grocery (Kroger, Albertsons, regional). Penetration math: **75% Walmart/Club, 25-40% grocery, 25-40% c-store.** The Costco consumer is very different from Maria. Don't over-index on the Walmart consumer."*

→ Pre-launch audience-build is **multi-channel-native, not Walmart-first then everyone else.** Three pre-launch tracks run concurrently:

| Track | Audience | Channels | What lands them |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Walmart-shopper track** | Maria + Donna + Garcias + Kim+Cal | TikTok-mom creators, school-newsletter, sideline sampling, parent-Reddit | Trial-risk-mitigation messaging — *"$5.99, same as Otter Pops, twice the nutrition"* |
| **Costco-shopper track** | Costco/Sam's $100k+ stock-up shopper | Costco demos (when available), Costco Connection ads, Costco-shopper YouTube reviewers (CostcoGuy4u, DealNews), Reddit r/Costco | Curation-trust messaging — *"the next better-for-you brand worth the cart slot"* |
| **Grocery + C-store track** | Kroger/Albertsons brand-aware label-readers + C-store impulse buyers | Geo-targeted Meta + TikTok in grocery-heavy metros, c-store category-buyer trade content | Different by channel — middle-aisle = label-led; c-store = brand recognition from prior channels |

**Pre-launch sequencing implication:** all three tracks have to be *warm before launch* — the Costco shopper needs Costco-native pre-awareness, not a TikTok-mom-creator video. Channel-native messaging in each track, but one consistent brand identity across all three.

## PART A — PRE-LAUNCH AUDIENCE PROFILE

**CHASE DIMOND:**
> "Pre-launch and launch are *two different audiences.* The persona matrix already shows it.
>
> The Tier-1 launch *buyer* is in the top-left quadrant — Maria, Donna, Pat. Walmart-only, in-store, heritage-leaning, stockup. These are who *buys the SKU on Day 1.* But they're also the **hardest-to-reach pre-launch** segment. They don't follow brands on Instagram. They're not on Substack. They don't do D2C subscriptions. Their *discovery* mechanism is the shelf itself — and the shelf isn't live yet.
>
> The pre-launch *amplifier* is in the bottom-right quadrant — **Jess, Megan, and the content-amplifier corners of Garcias and Ron.** App-first, multi-retailer, trend-leaning, brand-discovery-online.
>
> - **Megan** (millennial, label-reading, $60-90k) — *researches before she buys.* She'll find a pre-launch D2C site through a Substack / Instagram / parent-creator endorsement. She'll buy a small order, post about it, validate it for her network.
> - **Jess** (suburban dual-income, $90-130k) — Walmart+ app-first, multi-retailer. She'll be reached through paid Instagram + parent podcasts + Substack ad placements. Not a Whole-Foods shopper, but the discovery posture is the same.
> - **Garcias-younger-generation** (kids 10-14 with YouTube access, mom in her 30s) — discoverable through Hispanic-American kid-creator content (TikTok / YouTube), WhatsApp family-sharing, properly-translated Spanish content placements.
> - **Ron** (tween dad, sports-channel content diet) — discoverable through sports-creator content (Reddit-coded, ESPN-adjacent, AAU-sponsorship-adjacent).
>
> These four amplifier segments are *not* the brand's biggest revenue. But they're the **velocity signal** that makes the Walmart freezer launch work.
>
> Pre-launch goal: **3,000-5,000 households in the amplifier audience, captured to email + opt-in, with ~30% having sampled the product, before Walmart freezer slot goes live.** That's the number that creates a launch-week social signal.
>
> Pre-launch is **not a money-making channel.** It's a **velocity-priming channel.** Budget for pre-launch is *cost-of-launch,* not revenue-bearing."

**SHARP:**
> "Behavioral note on Chase's framing. The 3-5k household number is *small,* and that's fine. We're not building a D2C brand. We're priming a *Walmart-shelf launch.* The amplifier audience's job is to *signal scale,* not *generate revenue.* This is what Liquid Death did — early D2C buyers + content amplifiers + sampling at events + then mass retail. The D2C revenue was always small; the *signal* was the asset."

## PART B — CHANNEL ARCHITECTURE

**JULIAN:**
> "Five channels, ranked by leverage for this brand. Each takes a different lane."

### Channel 1 — Sports CEP Activation (highest leverage)

**INDIGO:**
> "Sports as Category Entry Point is the brand's single biggest lever per the phygital sports brief. Three plays:
>
> 1. **Sideline / Little League / AAU sampling.** Free pops at the field. Saturday morning soccer, after-game baseball, AAU regional tournaments. Sample-mom-and-kid simultaneously. Capture email at the sampling table (raffle for a year of pops + a $50 Walmart gift card on launch week). 200-300 events nationwide over 12 weeks pre-launch.
> 2. **Sports-creator partnerships.** YouTube + TikTok kid-sports creators. AAU coaches with content followings. The 65%-of-kids-learn-from-influencers stat from the deck. Not celebrity athletes — sub-1M creator partnerships at $500-2k each. 20-50 creators across basketball, soccer, flag football, skateboarding, climbing.
> 3. **Local Little League / sports league sponsorship.** $1-5k regional sponsorships. The brand's logo on the team banner. Plus sampling commitments. Builds *associative memory* — the brand is *next to* the game in the kid's mind by the time they hit Walmart shelf.
>
> Estimated pre-launch spend on Channel 1: **$50-100k.** Probably the largest pre-launch line item.
>
> Why it works: sports is the **bipartisan-by-design** activation. Red-state Little League + blue-state climbing gym + Hispanic-American AAU basketball — they all sample. The brand's bipartisan position is validated in *literal,* embodied form."

### Channel 2 — Kid-Creator + Parent-Creator UGC (high leverage)

**INDIGO:**
> "Parallel to Channel 1.
>
> - **Parent-creator network.** Megan + Jess discover brands through Instagram + TikTok + Substack parent-creator content. Engage 30-50 mid-tier parent creators ($500-3k each). Provide product + manifesto + reverse-mullet packaging early. Ask for honest review. Don't over-script.
> - **Kid-creator content from sports activations.** The kids at sampling events become content (with parent consent + likeness release). Authentic moments of *kid-loving-product* are the asset.
> - **The twist-off vs teeth-tear comparison content.** Built-in viral format. *Old way vs new way.* Single creator can produce this in 30 seconds; 50 creators can flood it.
> - **Mango Chamoy taste-test content.** Anglo-American kid trying Mango Chamoy for the first time = content. Hispanic-American kid eating Mango Chamoy at the grandmother's = content. Both formats viable.
>
> Estimated pre-launch spend on Channel 2: **$30-50k.** Volume of creators matters more than scale of any one creator."

### Channel 3 — D2C Pre-Launch (medium-low leverage, high-signal)

**CHASE DIMOND:**
> "Small D2C pre-launch site. Not a sustained channel — a 12-16-week capture window before Walmart launch.
>
> - **Pre-launch email capture.** Landing page with the manifesto + a single CTA: *Be the first to taste. Reserve a 12-pack from the founding batch.* $0 deposit or $5 deposit applied to first purchase. Goal: 5,000 emails pre-launch.
> - **Founding-batch micro-launch.** 12-16 weeks before Walmart, ship 500-1,000 units D2C at slight loss ($5-8 per box shipped, $5.99 retail). These are the amplifier-audience households who post.
> - **Email engine post-capture.** 3-email pre-launch sequence: manifesto / behind-the-scenes founder story / shipping notice. 8-email launch-week sequence: *find us at Walmart by zipcode* / *here's what's in pack* / *first reviews are in.*
> - **Subscribe-and-save (Tier-4 from audience tiers doc) is a small ongoing revenue tail** — Megan + Jess + Garcias-younger-mom who want predictable bulk. Maybe 5-10% of long-term revenue. Worth maintaining as the brand's owned-channel asset.
>
> Estimated pre-launch spend on Channel 3: **$15-30k** (site build + email tool + founding-batch shipping subsidy + minimal paid acquisition to drive email capture).
>
> The D2C site is the **brand's owned canon** for press, partnerships, and the Kinder's pitch packet. Even if commercial volume is small, the *existence* of a clean brand-canonical D2C site is a credibility signal Ishan walks into Kinder's with."

### Channel 4 — Selective Paid Social (medium leverage, narrow targeting)

**DEPESH MANDALIA:**
> "Not a paid-social-first brand. Mass paid social is wrong for this brand pre-launch because:
>
> (a) Tier-1 launch buyers (Maria, Donna, Pat) don't convert from paid Instagram. They convert from Walmart shelf.
> (b) Paid social pre-launch *without product on shelf* is asking for low-intent traffic that can't convert.
>
> BUT — narrow paid social *to amplifier audiences only* is high-leverage. Three campaign types:
>
> 1. **Instagram + TikTok paid retargeting** on parent-creator content. We don't pay for cold reach; we amplify the organic content that's already working. ~$500-2k per creator partnership for paid amplification on their post.
> 2. **Substack ad placements** — Megan + Jess subscribe to parent newsletters. Direct ad placement in 5-10 parent-focused Substack publications. ~$500-2k per placement.
> 3. **YouTube pre-roll on sports-creator content** — narrowly targeted to the sports-creator audience we've partnered with. ~$5-10k total over 12 weeks.
>
> Estimated pre-launch spend on Channel 4: **$25-50k.**
>
> Discipline: **no broad reach paid social pre-launch.** Wrong audience, wrong intent, wrong timing. Save the broad paid for post-launch when shelf is live."

### Channel 5 — PR + Earned + Owned Press (low spend, high credibility)

**JULIAN:**
> "Earned channel. Lower spend, higher credibility-per-dollar.
>
> - **Trade press** — *Food Navigator USA, Snack Food & Wholesale Bakery, Food Dive, Modern Retail.* The kids-snack-category trade press. Pitch the *category whitespace* angle. The deck's data is the story.
> - **Parent-facing press** — *Romper, Cup of Jo, Parents.com (digital).* Less Big-Pop-Mommy-blogger, more sharp-parent-content. The four-parity model is the press hook.
> - **Trade publication podcasts** — *FoodTech IQ, FastCo, How to Build a Brand* — Ishan + Davis interviews as the founder origin story.
> - **Investor / industry conferences** — Expo West, Sweets & Snacks Expo. Trade-show sampling + founder-pitch presence.
>
> Estimated pre-launch spend on Channel 5: **$10-20k** (PR retainer + conference fees + travel).
>
> Credibility-per-dollar is highest here. A *Food Navigator* feature article is more useful in the Kinder's pitch deck than $50k in paid social impressions."

## PART C — TRIAL MECHANIC (LOCK)

**INDIGO:**
> "Trial mechanic — get product in mouths before shelf launch.
>
> - **Primary trial mechanic: sports-event sampling.** Free pops at AAU regional tournaments, Little League games, soccer Saturdays, climbing gym youth events. Sample mom + kid + grandmother simultaneously. Capture email.
> - **Secondary trial mechanic: D2C founding-batch.** 500-1000 boxes shipped to the email-captured amplifier audience at slight loss. They post. The post becomes content.
> - **Tertiary trial mechanic: select trade-show sampling.** Expo West, Sweets & Snacks. Industry sampling = bypass for Walmart buyer + Kinder's principal validation.
>
> Trial volume target: **~20,000-40,000 sample units shipped pre-launch.** That's a 12-16-week budget commitment — but it's the velocity-priming asset that makes launch week click."

## PART D — D2C BRIDGE

**CHASE DIMOND:**
> "The D2C site is the *brand's owned home* for the audience the pre-launch courtship captures.
>
> Three states of the site:
>
> - **Pre-launch (T-16 to T-1 weeks):** Manifesto + email-capture + founder story. *Be the first to taste.*
> - **Founding-batch (T-12 to T-4 weeks):** Buy a 12-pack from the founding batch. $5.99 + shipping. Limited supply. Direct-to-fridge experience.
> - **Launch + ongoing (T-0 onward):** *Find us at Walmart by zipcode.* Plus subscribe-and-save for the amplifier audience that wants ongoing direct. Plus brand-canon content (recipes, behind-the-scenes, school-lunch ideas, sports-game packing tips).
>
> The site is the brand's *canonical voice* on the internet. Megan + Jess come back for content; Ishan + Davis use it for partnerships and press.
>
> Hosting + email + light-development: ~$10-20k pre-launch. Ongoing minimal."

## PART E — BUDGET SHAPE (THE FOUNDER'S NUMBER)

**JULIAN:**
> "Ishan needs a *budget shape* — not a final number — to walk into Kinder's principal pitch with.
>
> Total pre-launch spend (Channels 1-5 + trial mechanic + D2C bridge): **$130-250k.**
>
> Range justification:
>
> - Low end ($130k): Sports activation light (50 events), creator partnerships light (20 creators), D2C founding-batch minimal (500 units), paid social narrow (~$25k), PR/earned ($10k).
> - High end ($250k): Sports activation full (200-300 events), creator partnerships full (50-100 creators), D2C founding-batch substantial (1,000 units), paid social full (~$50k), PR/earned ($20k), trade-show presence at 2 events.
>
> **Recommendation:** Pitch Kinder's at the **mid-point ($180-200k).** That's defensible — meaningful pre-launch activation without burning launch-budget margin. Plus the *post-launch* trade-promotional budget (endcap, freezer-door real estate, in-store demo) is a *separate line item* — and the bigger one.
>
> **Critical:** the pre-launch budget is *cost-of-launch,* not marketing-budget. It exists to *make the Walmart launch hit velocity in week 1.* Without it, the brand can still launch — but it launches *cold* and the freezer SKU lives on a Walmart velocity-bar that determines its 6-month survival.
>
> Ishan walks into Kinder's with: *I need $200k pre-launch for the audience-build. Plus the launch-week trade-promotional commitment to Walmart. Together, we're protecting the launch.*"

**DEPESH:**
> "Confirm on Julian's read. The $200k pre-launch is the **insurance policy** on the Walmart launch. Without it, the SKU is alone on the shelf and the velocity-bar is up to the consumer's shelf discovery. With it, the SKU launches with a baked-in social signal and 3-5k email-captured households generating discovery content in launch week."
