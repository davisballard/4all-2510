## TYPOGRAPHY LANE (locked)

Three-register system. Same DNA across every brand surface — pack, ad, social, D2C, OOH.

| Register | Role | Spec direction |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary — Editorial Display Serif** | Brand wordmark + headlines + warm-direct copy | **Tiempos / Domaine / GT Sectra register** — contemporary high-contrast editorial. Heavy weights + tight kerning + bold color blocking = kid-friendly-confident, not Bottega-premium. *Tiempos Black at 60pt on a coral background reads kid-warmth, not fashion.* |
| **Secondary — Heavy Display Slab** | Flavor names + display-callout moments + kid-confident register | **Cooper Black / Heavyweight Pixar-coded display register.** Plays where flavor names live (front-of-pack hero) + occasional kinetic moments in ads. Bounce-out animation register per Giuseppe (Block 07). |
| **Body — Clean Grotesque** | Back-of-pack body copy + supplement facts (regulatory) + brand mission line + workhorse body | **Inter / Söhne / ABC Diatype register.** Neutral, legible at all sizes, no personality competing with primary + secondary. |

**Why this lane:** 18-brand audit confirms no kid-snack-freezer-pop brand at the Walmart price tier uses editorial display serif as primary wordmark. Annie's brushes it (extra-bold rounded serif) but stays warmth-coded. The lane is empty. Editorial serif = the brand's *quiet confidence* signature on a shelf otherwise built from script + rounded-sans + cartoon-display.

## CHARACTER CALL (locked)

**No illustrated mascot.** Brand personality lives in voice + type + color + flavor names.

### Why no mascot
- Character brands age (Massimo: *typographic brands compound*)
- A drawn mascot codes "kid-only" — undercuts the parent-direct H3 register
- Mighty Fundippa's mascot is friendly but cartoon-coded; not the *editorial-confident-warm* register the brand wants
- The flavor names (*Mango Chamoy* / *Spicy Peach* / *Watermelon Agua Fresca*) carry the kid-personality the brand needs without a persistent mascot

### What carries the personality instead
- **Brand voice on pack + manifesto** (Creative Brief lock — WITH-YOU / PLAIN / UNAPOLOGETIC)
- **Editorial serif wordmark** carrying the brand's quiet confidence
- **Flavor names in heavy display slab** carrying the kid-energy
- **Color stack on shelf** carrying the loud-wall fun
- **Pack-format graphic** (single tube illustration, no character, no mascot, flat/geometric)

## REVERSE-MULLET PACKAGING PRINCIPLE (locked + codified)

The unbreakable rule. Every SKU follows this. The system IS the brand.

### FRONT (Kid Territory) — 1-second read at 10ft

- Flavor color dominant (~90% of pack surface)
- Flavor name in **Heavy Display Slab**, all-caps or sentence-case, dominates center
- Brand wordmark in **Editorial Serif**, top-left, fixed position, ~12-15% of pack height
- Pack-format graphic (single tube w/ twist-off cap visible, flat illustration, single color, no mascot)
- Bottom info bar (12 ice pops · net wt · ages 4+) — small, legible, cream-on-color
- **NO claims, NO free-from icons, NO supplement facts text. NO USDA Organic / Non-GMO badges. NO health-claim stacks.**
- **Max 3 things compete for attention.** Brand wordmark + flavor name + appetite illustration. Everything else burns trust (Block 03 Set #1 Q2 100% hit — *cluttered burns trust harder than any signal*).

### BACK (Parent Territory) — reverse-mullet flip + read

- **Cream background**, consistent across all SKUs
- Brand wordmark + tagline lockup top-right (*SMALL BITES. BIG WIN.*)
- **Three benefit shields** in fixed grid (Mighty Fundippa precedent); each shield = icon + one-word claim + one-sentence explanation
- Supplement facts panel (regulatory, FDA-compliant type)
- Brand mission line, one sentence, base position
- Free-from icons row below shields — smaller, supporting receipts (no artificial colors · real fruit · with vitamins)
- Producer info (Made for Kinder's Foods) at the very bottom

**The grid is what makes this scalable.** Same fixed positions for wordmark, shields, supplement facts across every SKU. Same fixed grid across Ice Pop → Fruit Snack → Chip → Chocolate Milk (D-4 Kinder-shape scalability gate).

## SHELF STRATEGY (locked)

**Loud-wall stacked freezer block.** When 5 SKUs stack at the Walmart freezer door, the rhythm of [wordmark · flavor name · pop graphic · info bar] reads as ONE brand; the rainbow of flavor colors gives the loud-wall energy; the cream-back consistency carries the parent-recognition signal.

### Three feasibility gates (from competitor landscape doc — non-negotiable)

| Gate | Discipline | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Gate 1 — SKU color saturation matches the legacy wall** | Pastel-on-white pack will *physically disappear* next to Otter Pops/Popsicle saturation. SKU dominant colors must be vivid/full-saturation — coral, magenta, mango orange-red, teal, watermelon pink. **Reference: Cosmic-Bliss-level saturation, not Once-Upon-a-Farm-level restraint.** | Loud-wall feasibility |
| **Gate 2 — Editorial serif wordmark is the *only* visual quiet on the door** | Everything else is loud. On a 10ft pull, kid sees saturated color; on the 3ft approach, parent sees serif wordmark + cream back peeking around the corner. **Reference: Liquid Death's heavy serif "Mountain Water" subhead — quiet inside the loud.** | Parent-recognition |
| **Gate 3 — Cream-back consistency across SKUs is the second visual wall** | Parent who flips one SKU and sees cream + editorial serif + three shields gets recognition for SKU 2 onwards. | System defensibility |

## MANDATORIES & CONSIDERATIONS (carried + new)

Carried from upstream briefs:

- **D-3 Transparency as platform mechanic** — pack design *shows the comparison; doesn't claim the conclusion* (FI-4 in pack form)
- **D-4 Kinder-shape scalability** — the system must travel from Ice Pop → fruit snack → chip → chocolate milk → Wave 4 without identity rework. Massimo unit-system across all SKUs is the test.
- **D-6 Multi-channel pack format** — Walmart 12-count is the launch SKU; system has to scale to Costco mega-pack (30+ count) + grocery 12-count + c-store single-serve. Same DNA, bigger canvas (Costco end-cap reads at 15ft); same DNA, concentrated (c-store single-tube).
- **P-1 Reverse-mullet packaging** — codified in detail above.
- **P-2 Kid-joy gate** — never compromise the fun. Front-of-pack must out-fun the better-for-you category while staying clearly not shitty.
- **M-2 Cross-cultural without tokenization** (Garcias) — no sombrero / calavera / mariachi visual cues. Cultural signal lives in flavor names + back-of-pack mission, never in pack illustration.

New identity-specific mandatories:

- **VI-1 No illustrated mascot, ever.** Brand personality = voice + type + color + flavor names.
- **VI-2 Cream-back consistent across every SKU.** This is the second visual wall. Breaking it across SKUs breaks brand-recognition. Wave 2/3/4 SKUs inherit cream-back.
- **VI-3 Editorial serif is the brand wordmark across every SKU.** No SKU-specific wordmark variation. Wordmark is fixed-position, fixed-treatment, fixed-color (coral or brand-dark).
- **VI-4 Front-of-pack max 3 visual elements competing for attention.** Wordmark + flavor name + appetite illustration. Bottom info bar is small/secondary; doesn't count.
- **VI-5 No USDA Organic / Non-GMO / Certified-Clean badges on front-of-pack.** Back-of-pack only, if substantiated. Front is appetite + brand + flavor.
- **VI-6 Pack-format graphic discipline — single tube illustration, flat, single color, no mascot, no character.** Not photographic; not hand-drawn-organic; not animated. Geometric/illustrated, restrained.
- **VI-7 Brand color signal lives in two places only — wordmark + one back-of-pack benefit shield (VITAMINS IN).** Doesn't dominate; quietly unifies the rainbow front + cream back.
- **VI-8 Three feasibility gates non-negotiable** (saturation matches the wall · editorial serif is the only visual quiet · cream-back consistency).
- **VI-9 Block 11 SKU-1 sketch is the working spec; not the final art.** Final art = Phase 4 production-room work (Massimo + Fink + Indigo + Davis at Higgsfield).
