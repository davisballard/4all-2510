## CONTEXT

The Strategy Brief landed the comms problem cleanly: **shelf recognition before shelf encounter.** The four launch-tier personas (Maria + Donna + Garcias + Kim/Cal) have to recognize the 4 All Snacks logo when they see it at the Walmart freezer door so the shelf-pickup moment passes. Pre-launch comms is a **recognition engine, not a conversion engine.**

This brief turns that into an actionable architecture: **5 channels** (Sports CEP / Creator UGC / D2C bridge / Selective paid social / PR + earned), **named tactical bets** added by the transcript debrief (Nickelodeon / YMCA & Boys & Girls Club / sports snack-shed / mommy blogs / in-person activations), **persona × channel × KPI matrix**, and a **trial mechanic** (sports-event sampling primary) that breaks Maria's trial-risk equation.

## THE COMMS PROBLEM (restated from Strategy Brief, sharpened)

Comms must:

1. **Build pre-shelf brand recognition** for the 4 launch-tier personas in channels they actually trust.
2. **Capture an amplifier audience** (~3,000-5,000 households) who become the velocity signal on launch week — Megan, Jess, content-amplifier corners of Garcias + Ron.
3. **Trial-prime the launch-tier personas** so the shelf moment isn't a cold-start (sampling in person, especially for Maria + Garcias where trial-risk is highest).
4. **Generate launch-week social signal** (UGC, reviews, press) at scale enough to validate Joe's bet and Walmart's velocity-bar in week 1.

What comms is *not* doing pre-launch: direct conversion, paid-social-first acquisition, broad-reach campaigning, claim-based wellness selling.

## TRIAL MECHANIC (LOAD-BEARING per Block 12 + Audience Brief M-3)

Get product in mouths before shelf launch. Three layers, in priority order:

1. **PRIMARY — Sports-event sampling.** Free pops at AAU regional tournaments, Little League games, soccer Saturdays, climbing gym youth events. Sample mom + kid + grandmother simultaneously at the event. Capture email at the sampling table (raffle for a year of pops + $50 Walmart gift card on launch week). **200-300 events nationwide over 12 weeks pre-launch.** This is the single largest line item and the load-bearing lever for trial.
2. **SECONDARY — D2C founding-batch.** 500-1,000 boxes shipped to email-captured amplifier audience at slight loss ($5-8 cost per box shipped vs. $5.99 retail). These households post. The post becomes content.
3. **TERTIARY — Trade-show sampling.** Expo West, Sweets & Snacks Expo. Industry sampling = bypass for Walmart buyer + Joe validation signal.

**Trial volume target: ~20,000-40,000 sample units shipped pre-launch.** 12-16-week budget commitment but it's the velocity-priming asset that makes launch week click.

## MANDATORIES & CONSIDERATIONS (carried + new)

Carried from Strategy + Audience Briefs:

- **D-5 Friction-removal explicit** — channel content reinforces "no behavior change" framing for Kim/Cal especially.
- **D-6 Multi-channel platform mandate** — Walmart-shopper + Costco-shopper + grocery/c-store tracks run in parallel.
- **V-1 Positive-POV only** in all comms output. No "wellness left," no "unlike X," in any creator brief or paid ad.
- **V-2 Benefit not feature** — every piece of comms content leads benefit.
- **V-3 No "ritual" / aspirational vocabulary** — concrete, kid-anchored, grounded.
- **M-1 Bipartisan-by-design** across every channel. Sports CEP is the bipartisan validation channel; PR mix is bipartisan (trade + parent press, not political); no creator partnerships with politically tribal voices either direction.
- **M-2 Cross-cultural without tokenization** — Garcias-reach channels (Univision, Hispanic-American kid-creators, mommy blogs, WhatsApp content) require Spanish properly translated when used; never machine-translated; never Spanglish-as-flavor-branding.
- **M-3 In-store sampling is load-bearing** — confirmed and is the largest pre-launch line item.

New comms-specific mandatories:

- **C-1 Awareness > conversion.** Every channel KPI rolls up to recognition + brand-search lift, NOT direct conversion. Don't track CPL pre-launch — track aided brand awareness in launch-tier persona groups.
- **C-2 No broad-reach paid social pre-launch.** Wrong audience, wrong intent, wrong timing. Paid social is *amplification of organic creator content,* not cold acquisition.
- **C-3 No claim-based comms without Saul gate.** Structure/function claims (immune support, energy, focus) need Saul + legal sign-off before any creator brief or paid ad uses them. The brand benefit *(won't crash at 2pm)* lands without the claim *(vitamins for energy support)*.
- **C-4 Tactics not execution.** This brief is the strategic comms architecture. Per-tactic creative + per-platform deliverable spec is **Production Brief** (post-greenlight, $25-40k engagement). Don't bake creative-execution detail in here.
- **C-5 Kinder's-validation channel.** Trade press + trade-show presence is *not just brand-building* — it's *Joe-pitch validation.* The press article is what Ishan walks into the meeting with.
- **C-6 Velocity-signal not revenue.** D2C pre-launch is *not* a money-making channel. It's a velocity-priming channel. Budget for pre-launch is cost-of-launch, not revenue-bearing.
