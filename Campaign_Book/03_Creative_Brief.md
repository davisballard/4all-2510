## THE SINGLE-MINDED PROPOSITION (internal — agency compass, not consumer-facing)

> **The same snacks kids reach for — finally crafted for the kid you're still shaping.**

This is the **internal strategic spine** every creative piece ladders to. Consumers don't see this line; they see what derives from it — manifesto, tagline, front-of-pack copy, social, ads, OOH. The agency uses it as the gate: *does this piece carry the proposition? If not, kill the piece — don't rewrite around it.*

The consumer-facing **surface** of this proposition is the tagline: **SMALL BITES. BIG WIN.** — the SMP compressed for kid + parent vocabulary. The surface tagline carries the **compounding math** implicitly: small upgrades repeated across a kid's day compound into one big payoff across a childhood.

**Why this framing:**
- *"the same snacks kids reach for"* — honors product-truth (we didn't invent a category; we rebuilt one). Locks the Otter Pop substitution-into-routine mechanic.
- *"finally crafted"* — lands FI-1 (wellness left these parents, *we showed up*) + Davis's *Finally* signature beat. *Crafted* (vs *made*) signals deliberate care without invoking premium-coded vocabulary; the brand's product story without overclaiming.
- *"for the kid you're still shaping"* — lands V1 emotional core (the line McCabe called *"the sentence I could write to forever"*); reads warm-parent-direct (H3 register).
- **The surface tagline does the compounding work.** *Small bites* = each snack-upgrade is small; *big win* = the compounded payoff across a kid's day, week, year, childhood (FI-3 platform thesis — the kid's day, not the SKU line). One small bite is the input; the big win is the output of many small bites stacked across a childhood.

**SMP + surface tagline = strategic spine + consumer surface.** SMP for the agency room; tagline for the kid + parent vocabulary.

## VOICE (reconciled and locked)

### Voice principle (top line, Steel)

> **Loud in color. Warm in word.**

*(Updated from Block 08's "Loud in color. **Quiet** in word." to reflect the H3 warmth landing. The verbal register isn't Stripe-cool-restrained; it's warm-confident-restrained — a parent who noticed talking to another parent who noticed.)*

### Voice in one line

**The brand speaks like a parent who noticed talking to another parent who noticed.** Warm, plain, sure of itself, never preaching, never flattering, never lecturing. The brand assumes the parent already knows what the brand knows; we just say it out loud and remove the catch.

### Three attitudes (post-H3, from 04.8)

| Attitude | What it means |
|---|---|
| **WITH-YOU** | Not above-you, not below-you. The friend in your circle who pays attention. Standing next to you at the freezer door, not behind a podium. |
| **PLAIN** | Five-letter words beat twelve-letter words. *"Made well"* over *"thoughtfully formulated."* *"Better stuff"* over *"holistic nutritional value."* |
| **UNAPOLOGETIC** | Sure of ourselves, not embarrassed by what we're not. We use some sugar. We use small amounts of artificial. We're not all-organic. We don't apologize for any of it. We make the claims that are ours; we don't chase claims that aren't. Joe's instinct on framing: *"Nature Made"* not *"All Natural."* |

### Five voice rules (post-H3, sequenced)

1. **Loud in color. Warm in word.** — visual register loud + fun + saturated; verbal register warm + restrained + confident. The brand never shouts at the consumer about its goodness. The kid hears it in the flavor; the parent hears it in the receipts on the back.
2. **Convey the BENEFIT, never the FEATURE.** (Elliot 2026-05-19, locked #1.) Features = inventory; benefits = why anyone cares. *"100% Vitamin C"* burns; *"won't crash at 2pm"* wins. Like a clean investor deck — don't list everything you have, say what the purpose is.
3. **Acknowledge, don't preach.** The parent already knows the system is broken. We don't explain it. We just *show that we know they know.* The brand is the consumer's *coworker,* not their teacher.
4. **Positive-POV only.** Brand speaks from its own block. Never *unlike X.* Never *wellness left.* Never *finally a brand that.* Comparison is strategy-thinking, never output. Brand acts as if the competitive landscape doesn't exist in its voice.
5. **No implicit comparisons either.** Including *"same price as the snacks you were already buying"* / *"better than X"* / any *better* / *more* / *less than* where the answer to *"better than what?"* is *"someone else."* *Same shelf. Same price.* standalone is OK because it's our fact, no second object.

### Bipartisan-by-design gate (from Audience Brief M-1)

- Test every line: would Donna read this without flinching? Would Megan? Would Maria? Would Garcias' grandmother? **If any of them flinches, rewrite.**
- No MAHA-coded vocabulary. No coastal-wellness-coded vocabulary. No anti-Big-Food explicit. No tribal political coding either direction.

### Cross-cultural gate (from Audience Brief M-2)

- Spanish (when used) properly translated by native speaker, never machine-translated, never Spanglish-as-flavor-branding.
- Multi-generational imagery includes grandmother present in frame (Garcias).
- English-default on pack at launch (per Block 11 founder lock); Spanish content for Hispanic-American context (Univision, Hispanic-American mommy-blog, WhatsApp) is a separate vetted translation.

- "Premium" / "artisanal" / "craft" / "small-batch" vocabulary

## BACK-OF-PACK COPY GUIDANCE

Reverse-mullet — back is where the parent flips and reads. **Information density is intentional + staged** (per Block 11). Three jobs:

1. **Acknowledge the parent** in warm-direct register (the manifesto opener, condensed)
2. **Deliver the receipts** in receipt-language (benefit-coded, not feature-stacked)
3. **Land the win** at close (the signature emotional beat — *Finally.* / *A win for the family.*)

### Back-of-pack structure (working spec for downstream copy work)

```
[Opening line — warm-direct, manifesto-condensed]
[Product-truth line — same canonical phrasing across SKUs]
[Per-formulation benefit lines — 2-4 lines, benefit-coded]
[Closing — signature beat]
[Brand mark + tagline lockup]
```

>
> *Same shelf. Same price. Across the day, the little betters add up.*
>
> ***Small bites. Big win.***
>
> *A win for the kid. A win for the parent. A win for the family.*

### Per-formulation back-of-pack approach

Two formulations sketched in the deck (slide 28). Each gets a distinct benefit narrative on back-of-pack but the *structural shape* of the copy stays consistent.

| Formulation | Benefit narrative (back-of-pack) | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **General Health Boost** *(Ca · Mg · Vit D · E · K2)* | *"With calcium and vitamin D. The stuff bones actually build with. Fewer crashes. More real fruit."* — bone/growth/nutrition density framing, benefit-coded | *"100% Calcium · Vitamin D · Magnesium · Vitamin E"* (feature stack) · *"clinically formulated"* (claim) · *"supports immune health"* (FDA structure/function — Saul gate) |

Each per-formulation back-of-pack uses the SAME OPENING (warm-direct) + the SAME CLOSING (win-triplet + tagline) — only the middle two lines change per formulation. **One brand voice, two product stories, parent learns brand once and trusts forever** (Davis directive D-3 + FI-3 platform principle).

## MANDATORIES & CONSIDERATIONS (carried + new)

Carried from upstream briefs (Strategy + Audience + Comms):

- **D-2 Innovation = nutrition.** Creative honors the nutritional delta on back-of-pack; never positions packaging or distribution as the innovation.
- **D-3 Transparency as platform mechanic.** Back-of-pack carries pricing + nutrition + ingredient comparison legibly. Parent reads, decides.
- **D-5 Friction-removal explicit.** Creative reinforces *"no behavior change required"* — especially for Kim/Cal.
- **V-1 through V-4** — positive-POV, benefit-not-feature, no-ritual, no implicit comparisons (all locked above).
- **P-1 Reverse-mullet packaging.** Front = kid + parent-scan; back = parent-flip + receipts.
- **P-2 Kid-joy gate.** Never compromise the fun. Front-of-pack must out-fun the better-for-you category while staying clearly not shitty.
- **C-3 No claim-based comms without Saul gate.** Structure/function claims (immune support, energy, focus, recovery) need Saul + legal sign-off before any pack copy or paid ad uses them.
- **M-1 Bipartisan-by-design.** Every line tests against the four-personas-don't-flinch gate.
- **M-2 Cross-cultural without tokenization.** Spanish properly translated when used; multi-gen imagery with grandmother present (Garcias).

New creative-specific mandatories:

- **CR-1 Voice principle: *Loud in color. Warm in word.*** Visual register loud + fun + saturated; verbal register warm + restrained + confident. Steel's split principle (Block 08), updated for H3 warmth.
- **CR-2 Three attitudes locked: WITH-YOU + PLAIN + UNAPOLOGETIC.** Every creative output reads from these postures. Replace "Quietly Made Better" (Block 04 register) — that framing reads understated-Stripe-restrained, which doesn't match H3's warmth.
- **CR-3 Single-minded proposition is the gate.** Every piece of creative communicates the single proposition — *the same snacks kids reach for, finally made for the kid you're still shaping* — before anything else. If a creative piece doesn't carry it, kill the piece, don't rewrite around it.

- **CR-5 Front-of-pack maximum three things compete for attention.** Brand wordmark + flavor cue + one benefit line (singular). Plus the appetite illustration. *Cluttered burns trust harder than any other signal* (Block 03 Set #1 Q2 100% hit).
- **CR-6 Back-of-pack staging.** Information density is intentional. Health/free-from claims stay on back/side, never front.
- **CR-7 Locked canonical phrasings cannot be paraphrased.** *Snacks you used to grab. Now made for the kids of today.* / *Raising a kid is hard enough without negotiating snacks.* / *A win for the kid. A win for the parent. A win for the family.* — these are exact. Don't loosen the language; they earned their lock.
